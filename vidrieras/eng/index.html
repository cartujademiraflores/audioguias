The Flagellation

The foreground shows Christ bound to a column, His hands tied behind Him as two men lash Him with whips. To the right stands Pontius Pilate, beside him a red-clad figure some identify as Herod. Above, hidden behind the column, is the earlier trial before Pilate, which chronologically precedes the scourging.

The Descent from the Cross

The dead Christ is taken down and handed to His mother. Mary Magdalene and Mary of Cleophas bring oils and spices to embalm His body, while Nicodemus and Joseph of Arimathea aid in lowering Him, flanked by John. The compassion in the faces reflects the ideals of Flemish devotional art.

Pentecost

After the Ascension, Christ sent the Holy Spirit upon the disciples as a dove and tongues of fire. Mary, at the center, appears in prayer with a book on her lap. John is identified by the scroll at the edge of his cloak. All the disciples bear halos, signifying their sanctity.

The Last Judgment

The final scene portrays the vision from Revelation: the Son of Man appears among the clouds in great power and glory, seated with His right hand raised to judge. John the Baptist and the Virgin Mary kneel in intercession. A host of angels blow their trumpets, and the dead rise from their tombs as shrouds.

The Coronation of the Virgin

Mary is crowned by the Holy Trinity: on the left, God the Son with the Crown of Thorns and bare torso; on the right, God the Father with beard and red mantle, holding the orb of the world. Angels lift her skyward, foreshadowing the Assumption proclaimed in 1950.
